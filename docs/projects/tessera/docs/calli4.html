The full alphabet in hand-written form

Every character in canonical orientation (pixel grid, left) and its derived calligraphic stroke form (right). Rule priority — (1) closed border → FRAME; all-nine → SOLID; full top-two or top-two-cols → parallel pair. (2) Full-row and full-column triples fire next, each requiring at most one of its three pixels to be already covered. (3) 2×2 blocks among the remaining pixels become ovals. (4) Diagonal triples fire under the same overlap constraint. (5) Pixels adjacent to a covered pixel get a short connector. (6) Two-pixel orthogonal chains become short strokes; isolated pixels become triangular ticks pointing toward the centre. Diagonal adjacency never connects. Whenever the centre pixel is filled, a solid centre node is drawn over the strokes — marking the crossing.
