Topological defects

Each window is a small region of space whose boundary faces are glued to one another by a rigid motion, so light — and your view — leaves through one wall and returns through its partner, possibly rotated. Orbit a defect to watch its identification warp the world seen through it. The plaque beneath each window names the 3-manifold it stands for.

On mobile — scroll the column; tap a window to bring it to life. Drag to orbit, pinch to zoom. Tap ✕ (or another window) to stop it.
On desktop — windows tile into a grid. Click one to run it; click‑drag to orbit, scroll or trackpad‑pinch to zoom, ✕ to stop. Only one runs at a time.
